Recommended Death Notice Formatting

Information you may wish to include:

  • Photo (optional)
  • Full name of person (including "nickname")
  • Age of person at death
  • Date and residence at death
  • Date and place of birth
  • Parents, spouses, children and grandchildren's names
  • Previous residences
    • Ex: formerly of Flossmoor
  • Work history, military history, education
  • Clubs, hobbies or interests, accomplishments, volunteer activities
  • Survivors or those who predeceased the person including immediate family, relatives, close friends
  • Emblems (optional) are a great way to honor those that served our country or police/fire department or that had a strong affiliation to a particular religion
  • Funeral or memorial service information
    • Dates, times, locations, cemetery/place of burial location and contact information
  • Information on where memorial contributions or donations may be made
    • List full address and phone number if possible

Formatting Guidelines:

  • Use only standard abbreviations (examples: UCLA, USC, BA)
  • Do not write main text in all caps
  • Capitalize
    • Proper Names – Ex: Miller Funeral Home
    • Religions – Ex: Catholic, Jewish
    • Titles: When referring to a specific person – Ex: Mayor Richard M. Daley
    • Name of Individuals – Ex: John P. Smith
    • Organizations, Associations, Businesses – Ex: Girl Scouts of America
    • Formal, Named Mass Services
  • Commas
    • Use before and after the age of the deceased
      • Ex: John Smith, 88, of....
    • Use to separate date and time
      • Ex: Wednesday, 10 a.m. or Monday, Jan. 17, 10 a.m.
    • Use to separate survivors of the same relationship
      • Ex: brother of Ann, Carol and Pat
  • Maiden Name
    • Use “nee" when referring to the deceased
      • Ex: Mary Thomas, nee Smith,
  • Photos
    • Head shots are recommended
    • Photos will only run in color when applicable
  • Quotations for Nicknames
    • Use quotations to indicate a nickname on a death notice
      • Ex: Marion "Marty" L. Benard
  • Wording
    • Using the word "of":
      • Use the word "of" before the address or town when following the name of the deceased at the beginning of the notice.
      • Ex: John P. Smith, 55, of Springfield
    • Using the word "at":
      • Use the word "at" when services are held where the deceased is resting.
      • Ex: Services Sunday, 1:30 p.m. at Houser Family Funeral Home.
    • Using the word "from":
      • Use the word "from" when services are from the funeral home to the church.
      • Ex: Funeral Tuesday, Jan. 25, from Houser Family Funeral Home, 10045 Main St., to Saint Joan of Arc Church...
    • Using the word "to":
      • Use the word "to" when referring to the start and end time of services.
      • Ex: 3 to 9 p.m.
Note: We will run your notice as submitted by the Funeral Home or family unless contacted by one of our representatives. Listings subject to editing for style and grammar.
